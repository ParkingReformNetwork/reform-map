Summary

In downtown Chico, parking is no longer required for non-residential uses. Specifically, there are no minimum parking requirements for non-residential uses within the Downtown In-Lieu Parking Benefit Area, an area which covers the entire downtown. The relevant code section reads: "19.70.040 Number of parking spaces required.... G. Parking in the Downtown In-Lieu Parking Benefit Area. No parking is required for non-residential uses within the Downtown In-Lieu Parking Benefit Area. Parking for new residential uses within the Downtown In-Lieu Parking Benefit Area shall be one space per unit or as determined by land use entitlement." This reform appears to have been adopted on June 4, 2013, when the City Council adopted “Ordinance No. 2442, Ordinance of the City Council of the City of Chico Repealing and Replacing Chapter 19.70 Entitled "Parking and Loading Standards" of the Chico Municipal Code”. Previously, minimum parking requirements were imposed on virtually all uses within the downtown area, and these requirements were in effect as late as 2009.
